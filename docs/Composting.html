Composting

(How and Where to Do it)

Why Composting?

It reduces food waste! And, if you have a garden, you can use your compost as a natural fertilizer. If you buy/eat organic, and use organic produce in your compost, then the produce that you grow will also be organic!

How to Start Composting?

If you live in an apartment, and don't have a large garden/outdoor area, you can get a small composting bin. If you would like, you can also buy large composting bins.

What can I Compost?

  • Fruits and Vegetable Scraps
  • Coffee Grounds
  • Egg Shells
  • Compostable Tea Bags
  • Loose Tea
  • Non-dairy milk
  • Tofu
  • Nuts (but not walnut shells)
  • Anything that says "Compostable" on the packaging - i.e. some paper products - cupcake liners, plates, etc.

    • What do I do with my Compost?

    After you have composted your produce/food, you can use it as a fertilizer in your garden. If you don't have a garden to use your compost for, there are organizations on Pitt's campus that will take your compost!
